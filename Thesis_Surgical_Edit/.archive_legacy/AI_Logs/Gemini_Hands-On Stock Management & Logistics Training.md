From the start |

Let's begin with Inventory Control Calculations 🧮.

A fundamental concept in stock management is the **Economic Order Quantity (EOQ)**. EOQ helps a business find the exact "sweet spot" for how much inventory to order at one time. 
* If you order too many items at once, your **holding costs** (storage space, insurance) go up.
* If you order too few items, your **ordering costs** (shipping fees, administrative work) go up because you have to place orders much more frequently.

Here is your first drill. Imagine you manage inventory for a store selling campus hoodies 📦. Here is your data for the upcoming year:
* **Annual Demand ($D$):** 10,000 hoodies
* **Ordering Cost ($S$):** $50 per order
* **Holding Cost ($H$):** $1 per hoodie per year

The formula to balance these costs and find your optimal order size is:

$$EOQ = \sqrt{\frac{2DS}{H}}$$

Let's take it step-by-step. What do you get when you calculate just the top part of the fraction ($2 \times D \times S$) using our hoodie data?
